{% raw %}Decerebrate and decorticate posturing rigidity NCLEX review with mnemonic and pictures on how to tell the difference between the two conditions.<br /><br />Decorticate vs decerebrate posturing are two ABNORMAL types of rigidity associated with a brain injury. <br /><br />Let’s look at the difference between the two:<br /><br />Decorticate posturing: this is a type of flexed posturing and can indicate damage to the cerebral hemispheres.<br /><br />There will be adduction and flexion of arms and hands will be closed shut (flexed). The legs will be rotated internally and feet plantar flexed.<br /><br />Mnemonic for decorticate posturing: Remember the letters COR in the word decorticate for the word “core”. The patient will bring their ARMS to the core of the body (middle).<br /><br />Decerebrate posturing: this is a type of extended posturing and can indicate damage to the brain stem. This is the worst type of posturing between the two.<br /><br />There will be adduction and extension of the arms and pronation of the hands and fingers will be flexed along with extended legs and plantar flexion of the feet.<br /><br />Decerebrate posturing: Look at all the E’s in this word. There are a lot of them, so remember the word EXTENDED.
