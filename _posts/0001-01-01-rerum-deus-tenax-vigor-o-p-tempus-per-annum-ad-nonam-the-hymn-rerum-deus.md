{% raw %}The hymn “Rerum Deus Tenax Vigor” according to the Dominican Gregorian chant tradition. It is traditionally sung at the beginning of the office of None.<br /><br />Text: <br />Rerum, Deus, tenax vigor,<br />immotus in te permanens,<br />lucis diurnae tempora<br />successibus determinans,<br />Largire lumen (clarum) vespere,<br />quo vita numquam decidat,<br />sed praemium mortis sacrae<br />perennis instet gloria.<br />Praesta, Pater piissime,<br />Patrique compar Unice,<br />cum Spiritu Paraclito<br />regnans per omne saeculum. Amen.<br /><br />Translation:<br />O Strength and Stay upholding all creation<br />Who ever dost Thyself unmoved abide,<br />yet day by day the light in due gradation<br />from hour to hour though all its changes guide;<br />Grant to life's day a calm unclouded ending,<br />an eve untouched by shadows of decay,<br />the brightness of a holy deathbed blending<br />with dawning glories of the eternal day.<br />Hear us, O Father, gracious and forgiving,<br />and Thou, O Christ, the coeternal Word,<br />Who, with the Holy Ghost by all things living,<br />now and to endless ages art adored.
