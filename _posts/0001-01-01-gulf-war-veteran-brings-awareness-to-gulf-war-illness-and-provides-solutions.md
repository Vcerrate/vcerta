Shannon I’m an inspirational speaker, a veteran advocate and the author of “Battling the Storm Within” a deeply personal memoir that details my 20 year journey of living undiagnosed with PTSD, Post Traumatic Stress Disorder caused by MST, Military Sexual Trauma and GWI, the Gulf War Illness.<br /><br />The Gulf War Illness (GWI)  is a chronic multi-symptomatic disorder affecting returning military veterans and civilian workers of the Gulf War since August 2, 1990. <br /><br />There is a wide range of acute and chronic symptoms have been linked to it, the Gulf War Veterans’ Medically Unexplained Illnesses is a prominent condition affecting Gulf War Veterans that has a cluster of medically unexplained chronic symptoms that can include fatigue, headaches, joint pain, muscle pain, cognitive problems, rashes, digestive problems, indigestion, insomnia, dizziness, respiratory disorders, and memory problems. <br /><br />The VA refers to these illnesses as &quot;chronic multi-symptom illness&quot; and &quot;undiagnosed illnesses.&quot; The VA prefers not to use the term “Gulf War Syndrome” when referring to medically unexplained symptoms reported by Gulf War Veterans. <br /><br />If you or someone you know that may have served in the Gulf War starting from the Desert Storm War from 1990 to present contact closest the VA hospital nearest you.
