{% raw %}Sea level rise is one of the many threats we face as Earth's climate changes. Global average temperatures are rising, and so are ocean temperatures, forcing the water itself to expand. Higher temperatures in the arctic have also led to rapid ice cap melting, further adding to ocean levels.  Climate models suggest that for every 1 degree Celsius of future warming, water levels could rise another seven and a half feet in some spots.
