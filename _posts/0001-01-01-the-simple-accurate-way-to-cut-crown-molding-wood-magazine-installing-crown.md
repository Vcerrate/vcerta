{% raw %}Installing crown molding in your home or on a project? With this cutting technique, you use a simple jig and the built-in 45-degree miter stops on your miter saw--no bevel-cutting to get super-accurate joints. In fact, you can cut the compound angles even with a non-compound miter saw. The trick is to cut the molding in the upside-down installed orientation.
