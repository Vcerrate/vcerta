{% raw %}In the course of the First World War, mind-altering agents were used for both medical and non-therapeutic purposes. And they were issued by the authorities as well as self-prescribed by soldiers. Sedative drugs such as alcohol, morphine, snow-white, and opioids helped soldiers to subdue the physical and emotional pain, relax, and alleviate the horrors of combat but not without a cost.<br /><br />This was a time before drug regulation and awareness and things couldn’t have been different than any other war that followed.
