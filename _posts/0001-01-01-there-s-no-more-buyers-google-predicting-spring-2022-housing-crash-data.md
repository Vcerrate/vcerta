{% raw %}Data from Google Trends suggests a huge slow down in the US Housing Market. Is a 2022 Housing Crash Coming? Potentially in the Spring?<br /><br />Home buyer and real estate investor interest into the Housing Market SURGED during the pandemic in 2020-21. Data from Google Trends shows a large uptick in the searches of topics such as &quot;Real Estate&quot;, &quot;House&quot;,  and &quot;Zillow&quot;. <br /><br />However, over the last 4-6 months, Google Trends is showing a steep drop off in searches for these topics, suggesting that home buyers, particularly first-time home buyers, are on strike from the US Housing Market. <br /><br />GOOGLE TRENDS: <a rel="nofollow" target="blank" href="https://trends.google.com/trends/?geo=US">https://trends.google.com/trends/?geo=US</a><br /><br />The decline in home buyer and real estate investor searching on Google corresponds with a big drop off in New Home Sales, which after peaking in July 2020, have been on a steady trend downward over the last six months.<br /><br />The data for the &quot;Zillow&quot; search term is particularly instructive. There has been a 25-30% decline in searches for Zillow in recent months compared to the previous year. Interestingly, Zillow decided to shutter their homebuying program, Zillow Offers, around that same time.
