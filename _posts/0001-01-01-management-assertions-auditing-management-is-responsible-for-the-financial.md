{% raw %}Management is responsible for the financial statements, and management makes certain assertions (both explicit and implicit) in those financial statements.  For example, if the Balance Sheet says the company has $8 billion of inventory, then management is asserting that (a) the inventory actually exists and (b) the inventory value has been accurately calculated.  <br /><br />Management assertions can be grouped into 2 categories:<br />(1) assertions about classes of transactions and events (and related disclosures) for the period under audit<br
